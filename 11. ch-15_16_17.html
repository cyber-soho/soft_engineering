The Cost of Quality (CoQ)

Quality isn't free, but the cost of poor quality is often much higher. CoQ includes all costs incurred in the pursuit of quality or in performing activities related to the lack of it.

The Golden Rule: Costs increase exponentially as you move through the process. Fixing a bug in the field can cost 100x more than fixing it during design.

Reviews as a Filter

Technical reviews are the most effective filter in software engineering. They "purify" the work products (requirements, design, code) before they pass to the next stage.

Defect Amplification Model

A defect undetected in requirements can "amplify" into multiple defects in design and code.

Example: 1 Requirement Error -> 3 Design Flaws -> 10 Code Bugs.

Safety vs. Reliability

They are not the same. A system can be reliable (never crashes) but unsafe (causes harm).

  • Reliability: The software does what it is supposed to do. (e.g., The robotic arm moves smoothly).
  • Safety: The software does not do what it is not supposed to do. (e.g., The robotic arm never moves when "Stop" is pressed).
Hazard Analysis: Focuses on identifying potential failures that cause catastrophic loss (life, property) and eliminating them through design (e.g., hardware interlocks).
